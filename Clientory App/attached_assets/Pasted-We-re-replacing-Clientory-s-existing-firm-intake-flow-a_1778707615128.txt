=== STEP 4: REPLACE THE PROMPT GENERATOR ===

Delete the existing generator. Build a new combinatorial engine that produces exactly 10 prompts per firm, distributed across these 5 categories:

1. BRAND_DIRECT — 1 prompt
   "Tell me about {brandName}" OR "Is {brandName} a reputable {firmType} firm?" (pick whichever is more natural-sounding for the firm).

2. CATEGORY_GEO — 3 prompts
   Templates: "best {service} {firmType} in {city}, {state}" / "top {firmType} for {industry} in {city}" / "leading {service} firms in {state}"
   Use the HQ location and the firm's #1 and #2 primary services. Generate one prompt per service-location pair, varying template.

3. SPECIALTY_LONGTAIL — 2 prompts
   Templates: "{firmType} who handle {specialty}" / "{firmType} specializing in {deliverable} for {industry}"
   Pick the 2 most distinctive specialties/deliverables (most specific, least generic).

4. PROBLEM_SYMPTOM — 3 prompts
   Map deliverables to the triggering problem a prospect would describe. Seed a static map of 30 common deliverables across law / accounting / consulting / marketing. Examples:
   - "IRS audit defense" → "I got an IRS audit notice for my LLC, what should I do?"
   - "trademark registration" → "Someone is using my brand name, what are my options?"
   - "M&A advisory" → "We got an acquisition offer, who should we talk to?"
   - "sales tax nexus" → "I'm selling SaaS in 30 states and just got a sales tax letter, help"
   For deliverables not in the static map, call Claude Haiku to generate a realistic symptom query. Cache results.
   Pick 3 deliverables that produce the highest-signal symptom prompts (most specific, most distinctive). Avoid clustering on the same service area — spread across different deliverables for better coverage.

5. PERSONA_DRIVEN — 1 prompt
   Template: "I'm a {decisionMaker} at a {clientStage} {industry} company in {state}, who should I hire for {service}?"
   Use the firm's most common decisionMaker + clientStage + industry + primary service.

Generation rules:
- Reject and regenerate any prompt outside 4–30 words.
- Never include the firm's own name in CATEGORY_GEO, SPECIALTY, PERSONA, or PROBLEM_SYMPTOM — those test discovery, not brand recall.
- If the firm hasn't entered enough data for a category (e.g. only 1 distinctive specialty), redistribute the missing slot to PROBLEM_SYMPTOM or CATEGORY_GEO. Show the user a one-line note on the display screen explaining the substitution.
- The competitors field is collected but NOT used in v1 generation. Do not generate any COMPARISON prompts even if competitors are present.
- Because prompts are locked after creation, the generator must be conservative: prefer the highest-confidence template and variable choice for each slot. No experimental variants.

Note in code comments: this is v1 at 10 prompts, 5 categories. Architecture should accommodate (a) scaling to 100–200 prompts later, (b) re-enabling COMPARISON when competitor-driven prompts are added in v2, and (c) adding SERVICE_EXPLANATORY, VALIDATION, COST_PRICING categories.
